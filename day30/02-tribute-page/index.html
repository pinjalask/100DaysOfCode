Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam
Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "Missile Man of India," was an eminent scientist and the 11th President of India. His remarkable contributions to science, especially in the field of space and defense, and his dedication to inspiring the youth of the nation have left an indelible mark on India and the world.
Official portrait, 2002
Early Life
  • Born: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Family: Came from a modest background; his father was a boat owner, and his mother was a homemaker.
  • Education: Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later specialized in Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
Contributions to Science and Technology
  1. Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO):Played a key role in developing the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which deployed the Rohini satellite in near-Earth orbit.
  2. Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO):Known for spearheading India's missile development programs such as the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  3. Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests:Instrumental in the successful nuclear tests conducted in 1998, making India a nuclear-armed state.
Presidency (2002-2007)

Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "People's President," he was admired for his simplicity, humility, and vision for a developed India.

  • Advocated for "Vision 2020," aiming to transform India into a developed nation by focusing on areas like education, healthcare, and technology.
  • Encouraged scientific thinking and innovation among the youth.
Awards and Recognition
  • Bharat Ratna: India's highest civilian honor (1997).
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990) and Padma Bhushan (1981).
  • Numerous honorary doctorates from universities worldwide.
  • Named as the "Youth Icon of India" for his inspirational leadership.
Personal Philosophy

Dr. Kalam believed in the power of dreams, hard work, and determination. Some of his famous quotes include:

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

"You have to dream before your dreams can come true."

Legacy

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His life and work continue to inspire millions.

Memorial: The Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam National Memorial in Rameswaram stands as a tribute to his achievements and vision.

Why We Remember Him

Dr. Kalam's journey from humble beginnings to becoming a global icon of science and leadership showcases the power of resilience and dedication. He remains a symbol of hope and an inspiration for aspiring scientists, leaders, and dreamers worldwide.
